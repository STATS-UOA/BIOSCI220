Introduction to Module 3

This section of the course is due to run weeks 7--11 of this semester. As far as I'm aware at the moment that's from the 4th May--5th June.

This section of the course will be delivered completely online. By recommendation of the university there will be no "live" Zoom labs/lectures (due to bandwidth and timezone issues), rather, you as students are expected to go through the material provided and complete the required worksheets/assignments. As such I urge you to use Piazza to both ask and answer course related questions (we as the teaching team will do out best to answer all questions as quickly as we can---just not at 2am!). In addition, please feel free to email me at c.jonestodd@auckland.ac.nz if you want to discuss anything privately. If there is a common theme in the question relating to R I plan to run a midweek live coding session to work through any issues people may be having with R and their data analysis (date, time, and platform to be decided). In addition, I will be running two (or three, depending on demand) virtual office hours per week. These will be run via Zoom (link will be provided here) and be set up to be one-on-one sessions, you may be put in a virtual waiting room to await your turn during these hours.

Nicole, in Module 1, will have introduced you to the programming language R and Nick, in Module 2, will have talked you through some of the fundamental concepts of statistical modelling. Now we're going to bring the two together and get some hands on experience! We will be covering five topics during this section of the course. These are

  • Experimental Design
  • Visualising and analysis of multivariate data
  • Hypothesis testing
  • Introduction to linear modelling
  • Modelling II

Below you'll find all the materials for this section of the course. Prior to each week's lab I will be uploading videos and extra materials to help with your assessed lab work. There are 3 main sections below:

  • Lab materials, where materials for each week's lab will be available as well as details for the required assessed lab work;
  • Statslite, where short lecture style videos, associated slides, and lecture transcripts will be available for each topic;
  • Examples, where other useful code snippets and workbooks will be available.

As everyone is now working from home you'll need to use R on your own computer; I'd recommend using R through RStudio. Technically speaking R is the programming language and RStudio is, probably the most popular, an interface you can use to work with R. RStudio can be downloaded by following these instructions but remember you'll need to have R installed first (instructions here).

As with the course so far there will be a worksheet/assignment associated with each week's lab. These are examinable and count towards your final grade. Failure to complete on time will result in 0% for that work. Completion of each lab is expected.

Every week there will be a short (non-assessed) practise quiz put up on CANVAS. In addition, please feel free to give me (and the rest of the teaching team) anonymous feedback https://socrative.com/. The Room Name each time will be BIOSCI220. Before we get started please give it a go and introduce yourselves! Click here for an overview of this section of the course.
